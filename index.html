Scale at which
biology occurs

Cells operate on the nanoscale, with DNA, the genetic material, measuring only about 2 nanometers in diameter, and hemoglobin, responsible for oxygen transport, at 5.5 nanometers. Contemporary research emphasizes refining procedures and treatments to be more precise, personalized, and safe, like the bio-barcode assay, which efficiently identifies disease markers in blood samples, even when present in small quantities, without causing adverse effects.

Scale at which quantum effects dominate properties of materials

Particles within the 1-100 nanometer range exhibit distinct properties governed by quantum effects, including altered chemical reactivity, fluorescence, magnetic permeability, melting point, and electrical conductivity. Nanoscale gold, for instance, defies its typical yellow hue, displaying red or purple colors due to restricted electron motion. This property enables selective accumulation in tumors, facilitating precise imaging and targeted laser therapy without harming healthy cells, illustrating the potential of nanotechnology in medical applications.
