Abstraction

Nanotechnology refers to the scientific study and utilization of very small structures whose size ranges between 1 nanometer (nm) up to 100 nanometers. The term "nano" originated from the Greek word nanos which means dwarf. Nano denotes 10ยบ or one-billionth of a meter.

Various fields of study contribute to nanotechnology, including materials science, molecular physics, chemistry, biology, computer science, electrical science, and mechanical engineering. Unique phenomena occur at the nanoscale that is why scientists are so interested at imaging, measuring, manipulating, modeling, and utilizing nanoparticles (www.understandingnano.com).

Unique Features of Nanoscale

Nanotechnology involves operating at a very small dimension and it allows scientists to make use of the exceptional optical, chemical, physical, mechanical, and biological qualities of materials of that small scale.

Products of Nanotechnology in the Philippines

PhilGeneStrips is cost-efficient, easy to use in the field, and has the ability to properly identify those with the disease as well as those without. It can also detect White Spot Syndrome (WSS) virus. WSS is a viral infection which is contagious and can quickly lead to death. This innovation is very helpful for elimination of diseases in shrimps that firstly benefits the farmers.

Another innovation of nanotechnology in the country is the creation of water treatment system called the Portable Ceramic Water Filter (Pitcher Type). Through the principle of gravity, the two-liter capacity water filter is able to sift tap or deep well water using red clay with nanotechnology silver coating as the primary filtering agent (DOST 2015).
